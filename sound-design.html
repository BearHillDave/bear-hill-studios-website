Dave Cox

Sound & Interaction Design

Nearly 30 years of experience in creative technology, interactive design, and immersive audio — united by a belief that sound and experience are inseparable.

About Dave

The thinking behind the sound

Dave co-founded Lean Mean Fighting Machine in 2004 — a digital agency later acquired by M&C Saatchi. Since then he has developed products, produced audio, created brand identities, designed UX, developed XR experiences and worked in illustration and animation.

His guiding belief: there is a continuous line between sound, music, interaction and animation. All of it is about judging timing, weight, flow, space, surprise and balance.

Selected Work

A career built on immersion

Lean Mean Fighting Machine

Co-founded one of the UK's most creative digital agencies in 2004. Led interactive and brand work for major clients before the agency was acquired by M&C Saatchi.

Melody VR — Rescape Innovation

Spent several years specialising in Virtual Reality, becoming Product Director and designer for Melody VR — a clinically trialled wellbeing experience developed with Cardiff University that used music in VR to reduce anxiety. Results from the large-scale alpha trial were extremely positive.

Catmasonic — Important Small Things

Bear Hill Studios produced the voiceover for Catmasonic, made by former Rescape colleagues now at Important Small Things. A perfect example of how audio and interaction expertise combine.

Bear Hill Studios

Today Dave brings all of his experience to bear on creating immersive audiobooks and sound design for games, XR and digital media — always asking how sound can deepen the experience.
